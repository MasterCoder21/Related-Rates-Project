The Scenario:

A 202-foot long steel beam is being slid up the side of a building by a crane (It starts on the ground). The height of the left end of the beam off the ground increases at a steady rate of 2 feet per second. How fast is the distance between the building and the right end of the beam changing after 20 seconds of movement?

Note that the red line represents the steel beam.

Solving the Problem:

  1. Considering the problem as a triangle, X is the horizontal leg (against the ground), Y is the vertical leg (against the building), and the hypotenuse doesn't need a letter variable assigned to it.
  2. We're given the initial X value, which is 202, and (dy/dt), which is 2 ft/sec.
  3. We'll relate X and Y with the Pythagorean Theorem, a^2 + b^2 = c^2. This gives us the equation: x^2 + y^2 = 202^2
  4. Next, we take the derivative with respect to T (time), and our derivative is 2x(dx/dt) + 2y(dy/dt) = 0
  5. We can substitute 2 for (dy/dt) => 2x(dx/dt) + 4y = 0
  6. Rearrange => (dx/dt) = -2y/x
  7. We need to know the lengths of both the legs after 10 seconds. Because Y increases at 2 ft/sec, Y will be 40 ft after 20 seconds. Plugging this into our Pythagorean formula from the beginning, we get that X will be 198 feet after 20 seconds.
  8. We can now plug these values into our simplified derivative: (dx/dt) = (-2(40)/198)
  9. Simplifying this gets us the rate we want: (dx/dt) = -40/99 ft/sec

How do I use this animation?

  • Start Animation, Restart Animation, Reset Animation, and Stop Animation
    • Start Animation: Start the animation from where it stopped last.
    • Restart Animation: Resets the animation (and then starts the animation if it had stopped)
    • Reset Animation: Reset the frame count & current height of the metal beam
    • Stop Animation: Pauses the animation (does not reset anything)
  • Prev. Frame & Next Frame
    • Prev. Frame: Go back one frame
    • Next Frame: Go forward one frame
  • Animation Delay
    • Changing this amount will make the time between frames slower or faster, slowing down or speeding up the animation.
  • Frames Count
    • You may enter a value between 1,000 and 5,000 (inclusive). This value determines how many frames the animation goes through in each cycle. Setting this to a higher amount could potentially make the animation smoother (less choppy). Please note that 500 frames will be added on to clarify how the animation should play out completely.
